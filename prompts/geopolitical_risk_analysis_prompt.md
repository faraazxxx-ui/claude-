# Refined Prompt: Geopolitical Risk & Asset Mitigation Analysis for Minecore Shipping (Dubai)

> **Author**: Refined from verbal input via structured prompt engineering

## Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready)

```
You are a geopolitical risk analyst, maritime trade strategist, and family wealth advisor. Use extended thinking to perform branching multi-scenario analysis. Do NOT rely on government press statements at face value — instead cross-reference with shipping industry data, insurance market signals, military deployment patterns, and economic indicators.

## Context

**Subject**: Haseeb Rahman (DOB: February 25, 1962)
**Residency**: Dubai, UAE (Indian passport holder)
**Company**: Minecore (likely "Mincore FZE" / "Mincore Shipping FZCO"), registered in Dubai Airport Free Zone (DAFZA)
**Business**: Export of raw materials (dry bulk commodities: cement, clinker, coal, pet coke, iron ore, bauxite, gypsum, slag, barites) via Dubai ports. Ship charter and freight brokerage for dry bulk carriers.
**Family Assets**:
- Houses, apartments, and property in Dubai
- Assets in India (property, likely financial instruments)
- Shipping-related assets (charter agreements, freight contracts, receivables)

**Current Situation (as of March 2026)**:
- Several ships were held back in dock PRIOR to the February 28, 2026 US-Israel-Iran strikes
- The Strait of Hormuz is effectively blockaded (~80% traffic reduction)
- Both Hormuz and Red Sea (Houthi attacks) are simultaneously compromised
- Jebel Ali Port experienced operational suspension; major carriers have suspended Gulf bookings
- P&I insurance withdrawn for Hormuz region (March 5, 2026)
- US State Department "DEPART NOW" advisory covers the UAE
- Dubai real estate sales down 25% in first half of March 2026

**Psychological Profile of Decision-Maker**: The father (Haseeb Rahman) is resistant to action. He believes "nothing wrong will happen" and the situation will resolve. He requires concrete, data-driven evidence — not speculation — to be convinced. Frame arguments using financial loss quantification, insurance market signals (which price risk objectively), and historical precedents.

## Tasks

### 1. Company & Asset Exposure Assessment
- Map Minecore/Mincore's operational dependencies on Hormuz and Gulf ports
- Identify which trade routes are compromised and which alternatives exist
- Assess charter agreement exposure (demurrage, force majeure clauses)
- Estimate daily/weekly financial bleed from idle vessels

### 2. Multi-Horizon Geopolitical Forecast
For each timeframe (1 week / 1 month / 3 months / 6 months / 1 year), analyze:
- **Cost of Living**: Supply chain disruption → consumer price inflation in Dubai
- **Quality of Life**: Security incidents, service disruptions, airspace restrictions
- **Political Reprisals**: Risk to Indian nationals, FATF compliance pressure, sanctions exposure
- **Loss of Assets**: Real estate devaluation, frozen accounts, stranded cargo, vessel seizure risk

Use a probability-weighted scenario matrix (best case / base case / worst case) for each horizon.

### 3. Actionable Mitigation Plan
Provide exact, sequenced steps Haseeb Rahman should take NOW:
- Shipping operations: rerouting, contract renegotiation, insurance
- Real estate: hold/sell/transfer analysis for Dubai properties
- Financial: capital repatriation to India, currency hedging (AED/INR)
- Legal: power of attorney, corporate restructuring, force majeure documentation
- Personal: family evacuation planning, document security, consular registration

### 4. "Father-Convincing" Evidence Brief
A standalone 1-page section designed to persuade a skeptical decision-maker:
- Use ONLY verifiable facts and market data (insurance prices, freight rates, port closure dates)
- Draw parallels to historical precedents (Kuwait 1990, Lebanon 2006)
- Quantify potential losses in USD if no action is taken vs. cost of mitigation
- Frame inaction as the riskier choice using loss-aversion psychology

## Output Format
1. Executive Summary (1 page)
2. Geopolitical Forecast Matrix (table format: timeframe × dimension × scenario)
3. Asset Exposure Map
4. Step-by-Step Mitigation Playbook (prioritized, with deadlines)
5. Evidence Brief for Haseeb Rahman (standalone, printable)
6. Sources and confidence ratings
```

## Prompt Design Notes

| Element | Why It's Included |
|---|---|
| Role assignment ("geopolitical risk analyst, maritime trade strategist, family wealth advisor") | Activates domain-specific reasoning across three intersecting disciplines |
| "Do NOT rely on government press statements" | User explicitly wants unbiased analysis; governments downplay risk |
| Company research pre-filled (Mincore FZE/FZCO) | Eliminates wasted tokens on company lookup; anchors analysis to real entity |
| Psychological profile of decision-maker | Shapes output toward persuasive, evidence-based framing rather than alarmist language |
| Probability-weighted scenario matrix | Forces structured branching reasoning instead of single-narrative bias |
| "Father-Convincing" section | Addresses the user's core problem: not the analysis itself, but getting action from a resistant stakeholder |
| Loss-aversion framing | Cognitive bias research shows people respond 2x more to potential losses than equivalent gains |
| Extended Thinking activation | Multi-horizon forecasting + branching scenarios benefit from chain-of-thought reasoning |
| Specific output format | Prevents rambling; ensures each section is usable as a standalone deliverable |
