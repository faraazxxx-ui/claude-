# Charging Box Mounting Fix

## What Went Wrong

Your screws are spaced ~3.5" apart horizontally. The keyhole slots on the GE unit are only ~2.5" apart. That's a ~1" mismatch in width — the unit physically cannot slide onto the screws.

The vertical spacing (2.5") looks correct.

---

## Exact Fix — Step by Step

### Step 1: Get the Real Measurements

Throw away the AR Measure numbers. They're unreliable at this scale. Flip the unit over and use a physical ruler or tape measure:

- Measure **center-to-center** of the left keyhole to the right keyhole (width)
- Measure **center-to-center** of the top pair to the bottom pair (length)
- Write both numbers down

### Step 2: Mark New Holes on the Table

- Hold the surge protector against the underside of the desk exactly where you want it
- Poke a pencil or awl through the **large (round) end** of each keyhole slot to mark the wood
- Remove the unit and verify your marks match your measurements from Step 1

### Step 3: Fix the Old Holes

- Snap a toothpick in each old hole
- Dab of wood glue
- Let dry 30 minutes
- This gives the wood fresh grip nearby if the new holes end up close

### Step 4: Drill & Drive New Screws

- Drill pilot holes at your new marks
- Use **pan-head screws** (#8 x 3/4") — flat-head countersink screws slip through keyholes
- Drive them in leaving **3/16" (~5mm) of the head protruding** — this is the critical dimension for keyholes to lock

### Step 5: Mount It

- Align the **wide end** of each keyhole over its screw
- Push the unit up against the table
- Slide it toward the **narrow end** of the keyholes to lock
- It should click snug with no wobble

---

## Measurement Summary

| | Width (horizontal) | Length (vertical) |
|---|---|---|
| **Your screws** | ~3.5" | ~2.5" |
| **Keyhole slots** | ~2.5" | ~2.5" |
| **Mismatch** | **~1" too wide** | OK |

**One new pair of holes at the correct width and you're done.**
